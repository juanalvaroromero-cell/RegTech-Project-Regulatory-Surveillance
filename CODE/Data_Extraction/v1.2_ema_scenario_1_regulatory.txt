TITLE: Human regulatory: overview

=== TEXT CONTENT ===
Some regulatorytopics span the medicinal product lifecycle. This website offers an overview page for these topics, with links to relevant information in the product lifecycle stages.

Regulatory information onherbal medicinesandmedical devicesis contained separately, as these products are regulated differently in Europe.

For further information on EU legislation and procedures for the regulation of human medicines, see volumes 1-4 and 9-10 of therules governing medicinal products in the EU.

EMA provides incentives and support for micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in order to promote innovation and the development of new medicines.

Information about EMA's fees and how to pay

EMA has several websites and systems to support its work. Some of the information in these systems is available to all visitors, such as details about clinical trials in the EU, reports of suspected side effects, and other medicine-related data.

Learn about the step-by-step evaluation process for new medicines via the centralised procedure, from initial assessment to possible re-examination.
